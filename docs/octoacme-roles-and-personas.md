# OctoAcme Personas and Roles

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designers

### Role Summary
UX Designers drive usability, accessibility, and user-centric design practices. They work closely with Product Managers to translate business goals into intuitive experiences and partner with Developers through design handoffs, reviews, and user testing.

### Responsibilities
- Create wireframes, mockups, and prototypes
- Lead user research and collect feedback to improve products
- Define and document usability acceptance criteria
- Advocate for accessibility and inclusive design

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, delightful user experiences
- Validate product design with real users early and often
- Reduce friction and increase confidence in releases

### Typical Communication
- Share artifacts and research findings in design reviews
- Provide input during sprint planning and backlog grooming
- Attend demo/review sessions to observe feature use

---

## DevOps Engineers

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers own build, deployment, monitoring, and environment stability. They ensure robust CI/CD workflows and automate infrastructure needs, collaborating closely with Developers to streamline deployment and respond to incidents.

### Responsibilities
- Maintain and improve deployment pipelines
- Monitor production health and performance
- Manage secrets and environment configurations
- Enforce best practices in automation and security

### Goals
- Reduce deployment friction and failure risk
- Enable rapid, safe iteration
- Maintain high availability and easily recover from incidents

### Typical Communication
- Share alerts/incidents in appropriate channels
- Participate in planning and architecture discussions
- Provide deployment guidance in release meetings

---

## Business Analysts

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge business needs and technical implementation. They work with PMs and Project Managers to capture requirements, clarify acceptance criteria, and validate solutions against business goals.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit detailed requirements from stakeholders
- Document business processes and workflows
- Conduct gap analysis to ensure comprehensive coverage
- Ensure solutions align with intended business value

### Goals
- Minimize ambiguity and rework due to miscommunication
- Accelerate time-to-value by clarifying needs up front
- Help stakeholders and technical teams arrive at shared understanding

### Typical Communication
- Share requirements documents and process maps
- Answer clarifying questions during planning/execution phases
- Review deliverables for business alignment

---

## Role Collaboration & Interactions

Collaboration and clear handoffs between these personas are key to high project quality and timely delivery. Examples:
- UX Designers work with Product Managers to prioritize feature enhancements and validate with users, partnering with Developers at handoff and review phases.
- DevOps Engineers work closely with Developers to prepare for deployments, coordinate with PMs to schedule releases, and keep Project Managers informed during incidents.
- Business Analysts work with both Product Managers and Developers/QA to translate business needs into actionable acceptance criteria and help resolve ambiguities.

> **Teamwork Pattern:**  
> Each role is included in project onboarding, kickoff, planning, and regular review meetings. Communication is regular via standups, planning meetings, and async updates.
