# OctoAcme Project Management Processes

This repository centralizes project management processes and knowledge for OctoAcme, making workflows versioned, searchable, and accessible for all team members. It captures core organizational insights and best practices, reduces onboarding time, and ensures that project execution remains consistent and repeatable.

---

OctoAcme’s project management process provides a structured, yet adaptable framework for organizing cross-functional teams to deliver product features and improvements with a strong emphasis on clarity, collaboration, and continuous improvement. The approach begins with project initiation, where new ideas are validated through clear problem statements, stakeholder alignment, and well-defined success metrics. Each project is overseen by key roles such as the Project Manager (PM), who coordinates activities and risks, and the Product Manager (PdM), who defines outcomes and prioritizes work. Developers focus on implementation and quality, while stakeholders provide input and approvals. These core personas are clearly defined, ensuring responsibilities are well understood and all processes are repeatable.

OctoAcme’s workflows guide projects from inception to delivery via distinct phases: Initiation (setting problem and outcome), Planning (breaking down deliverables, estimating, and prioritizing), Execution (building, testing, and tracking progress), Release (deploying and verifying), and Continuous Improvement (retrospectives and action item tracking). Projects utilize visual tools like GitHub Projects boards with standardized workflow columns (Backlog, Ready, In Progress, etc.), and enforce clear pull request conventions with acceptance criteria, issue linking, and required reviews before merge. Risks and dependencies are tracked through risk registers, which are actively maintained and reviewed during regular syncs.

Communication is a central pillar, facilitated by scheduled cadences: daily standups or twice-weekly meetings for rapid alignment, weekly PM and product syncs, and monthly stakeholder updates. Escalation paths for blockers are clearly defined, ensuring issues move promptly from the delivery team to product leaders and sponsors when needed. Documentation and transparency are maintained with project artifacts—one-pagers, roadmaps, backlogs, risk registers, and release notes—serving as the single source of truth and reference in communication.

Quality assurance practices are deeply integrated at each stage. Automated tests, linting, and security scanning are required in CI pipelines; manual QA and demo sessions are used for feature acceptance; and deployment follows detailed, checklist-driven processes—including pre-release validation, smoke testing, and incident playbooks for rollback. After key milestones, retrospectives foster a culture of continuous improvement, capturing learnings, generating actionable follow-ups, and measuring the impact of changes for ongoing organizational effectiveness.
